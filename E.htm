Petra - The Rose City

Petra, also known as the Rose City, is a historical and archaeological city located in southern Jordan. It is famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Petra was the capital of the Nabatean Kingdom and later became a major trading hub in the ancient world.

One of the most iconic structures in Petra is the Treasury, carved into the pink sandstone cliff. Other notable sites include the Monastery, the Roman Theater, and the Royal Tombs. Visitors can explore the ancient city on foot or by horseback.

Aside from its historical significance, Petra is also known for its stunning natural beauty. The surrounding desert landscape adds to the allure of the city, making it a popular destination for tourists and archaeologists alike.
